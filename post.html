L’Oreal and Safety:

Why we test.

L’Oreal is first and foremost committed to its consumers’ safety. Traditionally it was animal testing that allowed us to assess the safety of our products and our ingredients. Thanks to our research in alternative methods to animal testing, particularly in the area of reconstructed tissue development, L’Oreal was able to end testing for finished products (as early as 1989) without making our products any less safe.

However, in those rare cases when we introduce a new ingredient into a product, our alternative methods for safety assessment (which aren’t animal testing) don’t always provide us with enough information to guarantee safety for our consumers. So the only way to obtain that information is to perform some animal testing.
However our advances in alternative methods have allowed us to slowly but surely close that gap. In fact, today testing on animals isperformed in less than 1% of cases. And we will continue to invest in the science of reconstructed tissue and predictive evaluation until we have fully eliminated the need for any testing on animals, without ever compromising on safety.

And for those who question why L’Oreal would need new ingredients, it is important to remember that they are needed to replace those ingredients that scientific experts consider risky to consumers or to the environment. But also we replace older ingredients by more effective ones. This is for example the case of new sunscreen filters in our sunscreens that prevent cell damage leading to skin cancer.
