Leading the way towards ending
animal testing.

L’Oreal is committed above all to its consumers’ safety. Thanks to our research in alternative methods to animal testing, particularly in the use of reconstructed tissue, L’Oreal is leading the way towards ending animal testing. Today testing on animals represents less than 1% of cases. We will continue to invest in the science of reconstructed tissue and predictive evaluation until we have fully eliminated the need for any testing on animals, without ever compromising on safety.

L’Oreal and Safety

L’Oreal is first and foremost committed to its consumers’ safety.

Traditionally it was animal testing that allowed us to assess the safety of our products and our ingredients. Thanks to our research in alternative methods to animal testing, L’Oreal was able to end testing for finished products (as early as 1989) without making our products any less safe.

Our commitment to ending animal testing

We’re committed to ending testing - over the past 20 years, the Group has invested €600M in research and production of reconstructed tissues, and employs more than 100 scientists in this area of research. Read more.

Our laboratories have contributed to most of the replacement
methods accepted today in the cosmetics industry. But we won’t be satisfied until we’ve eliminated the need for any animal testing.
